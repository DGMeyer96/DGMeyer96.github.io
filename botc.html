Breath of the Coyote

This project was created for the CSUSB CSE Game Design class and competition. 11 teams were given 10 weeks to design and develop a game of their choice. On the 10th week each of the teams demonstrated the games in front of a panel of judges and a winner was decided. The game's design was of my creation and I served as Project Lead for this project as well as Programmer and 3D Artist.

How To Play

The game is compromised of 3 challenges, each designed to showcase one of the core systems of the game. The game starts in a village where the player is tasked with the goal of collecting 3 trophies to prove they are worthy to become the new village chieftain. The challenges, the Trial of the Mind, Trial of Strength, and Trial of Agility, each reward the player with a trophy upon completion. Once all 3 trophies are collected the player returns to the village to complete the game.

The Trial of the Mind showcases the puzzle system where the player must light 3 magical pillars using fireballs.

The Trial of Strength showcases the combat system where the player has to defeat each of the enemy types to unlock the trophy chest.

The Trial of Agility showcases the movement system where the player is presented a series of platforms moving and shifting that need to be traversed to reach the chest at the top platform.

Gameplay

There are 3 core systems that make up the gameplay loop of the game: Movement, Combat, and Puzzle. Each system is independent of one another but also provide synergy when combined. There are 3 challenges (each associated with a system) that players mst complete before returning to the village to complete the game.

The movement system is relatively straight forward and encompasses all player controls and input. Camera movement is handled via Cinemachine which allows for a more natural camera feel. Movement is controlled via Unity's Character Controller to allow for proper movement physics.

The combat system is handled via a combo system. Players can time attacks to perform a combo or just click once for a single attack. There are 3 types of enemies in the game: a small, light imp, a mage imp, and a large, heavy imp. Each of the enemies has different health values and attack patterns.

The puzzle system manages has two elements: magic pillars and fireballs. Players use the fireball to activate magical pillars that are scattered throughout an arena. Once all of the pillars are activated a chest is opened.

Development

Development was done in Unity using the Lightweight Render Pipeline, Cinemachine, Shader Graph, and VFX Graph.

As Project Lead I was in charge of managing the GitHub Repository and weekly SCRUM meetings among the programmers. During each meeting the programmers would update me on their progress and I would assign new weekly goals as the project progressed.

As 3D Artist I created the majority of the 3D models for the game with the exception of the player model which was provided by a fellow student at CSUSB, JC Mariscal. The models were created using Cinema4D with texture work done in Adobe Photoshop.

As Programmer I worked on the game's back-end, audio, and graphics. For the back-end work I managed the project settings, programmed the game's UI, menus, settings menu, loading and scene transitions, as well as some work on the combat system. In the audio department I created the BGM and SFX playback with audio transitions for the battle and exploration track. For the game's graphics I setup the post-processing pipeline and lighting model. The game used a procedural sky through Unity's Post-Processing pipeline. Finally the game also features cel-shading and a cartoon outline with a posterizing effect through a custom render pipeline asset as well as custom shaders developed through Unity's Shader Graph.
